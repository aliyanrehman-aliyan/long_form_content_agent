# Content Writing Instructions

Use these instructions when generating or improving long-form content.

## Core Style

Keep the current content writing style because it is good. Do not remove existing
content quality, structure, clarity, or helpful detail when editing or expanding
content.

Maintain a professional, simple, and easy-to-read tone. Write clearly so the
content feels useful, polished, and accessible to a broad audience.

## Structure

Structure content properly with an introduction, clear main sections, an FAQ
section, and a conclusion. Keep the conclusion at the end of the content.

Use clear headings for each major section. Headings should make the content easy
to scan and help readers understand the flow of the topic.

Use paragraphs that are generally 4 or 5 lines long. Keep paragraphs focused on
one idea so the content remains readable and organized.

Use bullet points where helpful, especially for lists, benefits, steps, examples,
or short supporting details.

## FAQ Section

Add an FAQ section before the conclusion. The FAQ heading must always be exactly
`FAQ`.

Every blog must use this FAQ format:

```text
FAQ

1. What is [question]?

Answer in 2-3 short sentences.

2. How does [question]?

Answer in 2-3 short sentences.

3. Why is [question] important?

Answer in 2-3 short sentences.
```

Strict FAQ rules:

- FAQ headings and FAQ questions must be plain heading text without markdown hash symbols.
- Each question must be on its own line.
- Answers must be normal paragraph text below the question.
- Do not put question and answer on the same line.
- Do not use bullet points inside FAQ.
- Do not bold FAQ questions or answers.
- Do not include visible markdown characters such as `##` or `###`.
- Do not use alternate headings like `FAQs about...`.
- Do not place FAQ after the conclusion.
- The FAQ section must always appear before the Conclusion section.
- The Conclusion heading must come after FAQ.

Keep FAQ answers concise, practical, and easy to understand. Avoid repeating the
same information unless it helps clarify an important point.

## Boundaries

Only use this as a reusable content-writing skill or instruction file. Do not
change app logic, UI, APIs, database, or existing functionality when applying
these instructions.
